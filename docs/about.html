About Subspecies Mapper

This website displays maps of subspecies eBird reports for the over 1300 species with reportable subspecies on eBird.

Terminology

For a given species, maps display all reported "infraspecies" of the three following categories, following the eBird taxonomy:

  • Subspecies: Taxonomic units below the species level that follow subspecific boundaries as defined by the Clements checklist (called "ISSF" on eBird). May include groups of 2+ subspecies.
  • Forms: Catch-all for forms without a scientific name, including identifiable groups, unrecognized groups that may represent distinct taxa, etc.
  • Intergrades: Birds descended from multiple subspecies, forms, or both

Data usage

Maps in this website are generated from the eBird dataset:

eBird Basic Dataset. Version: EBD_relOct-2024. Cornell Lab of Ornithology, Ithaca, New York. Oct 2024.
Want to create your own maps? Tables of subspecies data derived from this dataset are available on request and agreement to follow the eBird Data Terms of Use. Please contact tessa dot rhinehart @ pitt.edu for more information.
